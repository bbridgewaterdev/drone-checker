Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about DroneChecker

Flying conditions
How accurate is the fly rating?

The fly rating is advisory only and based on live weather data combined with your drone's published wind resistance specifications. It checks wind speed, gusts, visibility, precipitation type and geomagnetic activity.

Ratings: Good to Fly conditions are within safe limits for your drone. Fly with Caution means one or more factors are marginal. Do Not Fly means conditions exceed your drone's safe operating limits.

Always use your own judgement and verify conditions on the ground before flying. The pilot in command is solely responsible for safe operations at all times.

Why does light drizzle show as caution rather than good to fly?

Most consumer drones — including all DJI Mini and Mavic models — have no water resistance rating. Even light drizzle can cause water ingress into motors, ESCs and camera gimbals, potentially causing permanent damage or a flyaway.

We flag drizzle as caution rather than good to fly because the risk is real, even if the drone doesn't immediately fail. Fly in drizzle at your own risk.

What does the KP index mean and why does it matter?

The KP index measures geomagnetic activity caused by solar wind interacting with Earth's magnetic field. For drone pilots this matters because high KP levels can cause compass errors and GPS inaccuracies, increasing the risk of flyaways.

KP 0–3: No impact on drone systems.
KP 4–5: Possible minor compass drift — fly carefully and stay close.
KP 6+: GPS may be unreliable. DroneChecker will show a Do Not Fly rating at KP 6 or above.

Data is sourced live from NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center.

How do I change wind speed thresholds for my drone?

DroneChecker uses the manufacturer's published wind resistance specifications for each drone model. Use the drone selector at the top of the dashboard to switch between models — the fly rating thresholds update automatically.

If your drone isn't listed, choose the closest matching generic profile (Light drone under 250g or Heavy drone over 250g).

Weather & data
Where does the weather data come from?

Weather data is provided by Open-Meteo, which uses ECMWF (European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts) model data — the same high-quality forecast model used by professional meteorologists. It's updated hourly.

KP geomagnetic index data is sourced live from NOAA's Space Weather Prediction Center.

How often does the weather update?

DroneChecker fetches fresh weather data every time you open the app and then automatically refreshes in the background every 30 minutes while the app is active. You can also tap ↻ Refresh in the header at any time to force an immediate update.

The header shows "Updated X mins ago" so you always know how current the data is.

Does it work offline?

Yes — DroneChecker caches the last known weather data on your device. If you lose signal at a flying site, you'll still see the most recently loaded conditions with a banner indicating it's offline data.

The golden hour calculator works entirely offline as it's calculated astronomically from your location coordinates, with no internet required.

Location
Why does the app show London when I first open it?

On first launch, DroneChecker shows London conditions immediately so you're never looking at a blank screen. Within a few seconds it should update automatically to your actual location once GPS or network location resolves.

If it stays on London, tap the 📍 button next to the search bar to manually request your location, or type your postcode or town into the search bar.

How do I save a favourite location?

Search for or navigate to a location, then tap the ☆ star button next to the search bar. The location is saved to your device and appears as a pill below the search bar for one-tap access. You can save up to 8 favourite locations.

Can I use DroneChecker outside the UK?

Yes — the weather, fly rating, golden hour and KP index all work globally. Weather data covers the entire world via Open-Meteo.

The CAA rules and guidance in the Checklist tab are UK-specific. If you're flying outside the UK, always check your local aviation authority's regulations — drone rules vary significantly between countries.

App & account
Is DroneChecker free?

Yes — DroneChecker is completely free with no adverts, no account required and no data collected. All features are available to all users.

Is DroneChecker available on iPhone?

DroneChecker isn't currently on the Apple App Store, but you can install it directly from Safari on iPhone or iPad:

1. Open dronechecker.co.uk in Safari
2. Tap the Share button ⎋ at the bottom
3. Tap Add to Home Screen
4. Tap Add

The app will appear on your home screen and behave just like a native app.

Is my flight log data saved? What happens if I uninstall the app?

Your flight log is saved locally on your device and persists across app restarts and phone reboots. It will not be lost if you simply close the app.

However, uninstalling the app or clearing the app's storage in your phone settings will erase your flight log. We strongly recommend using the Export to CSV button regularly to save a backup — the CSV file opens in Excel and can be stored in your photos or cloud storage.

Does DroneChecker collect any personal data?

No. DroneChecker does not collect, store or share any personal data. No account is required. Your location is only used to fetch weather data and is never transmitted to our servers. All data — favourites, flight log, settings — is stored locally on your device only.

We use Google Analytics to understand anonymous usage patterns (which tabs are used, general location region). No personally identifiable information is ever collected.

How do I report a bug or give feedback?

Tap Send Feedback in the app footer on the Dashboard tab, or email us directly at bbridgewater.dev@gmail.com. Please include your device model, browser or app version, and a description of the issue.
