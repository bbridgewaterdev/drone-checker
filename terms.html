2. Description of Service

DroneChecker is an informational application providing:

  • Live weather conditions and flying condition ratings tailored to your drone model
  • 6-hour hourly weather forecast with per-slot flying condition ratings
  • Wind direction, speed and gusts at 10m, 80m and 120m altitude
  • Geomagnetic activity (KP index) information from NOAA, integrated into the fly rating
  • Golden hour and blue hour calculations for aerial photography
  • Pre-flight checklist for drone pilots
  • Flight logging with CSV and Excel export for personal records
  • General UK CAA regulatory guidance
  • Links to official airspace information resources
  • Offline mode showing last known conditions when no internet connection is available
  • Favourite location saving for frequently visited flying sites

DroneChecker Pro is an optional paid subscription (£3.99/month or £29.99/year) providing additional features including:

  • Extended 72-hour hourly forecast and 7-day best day to fly planner
  • Ground hazard and indicative airspace restriction map
  • Cloud sync of flight logs and saved locations across devices
  • Multiple custom drone profiles with configurable wind thresholds
  • Today's conditions summary

5. Regulatory Compliance

It is your responsibility to comply with all applicable regulations including:

  • UK Civil Aviation Authority (CAA) drone regulations
  • CAA Operator ID registration — required for drones 250g or more, or 100g–250g if fitted with a camera (from 1 January 2026; optional for 100g–250g drones without a camera). Annual fee, currently £12.34 — verify on the CAA website as fees are subject to change.
  • CAA Flyer ID — required for all drones 100g or more (free)
  • Remote ID broadcast — mandatory for UK1, UK2 and UK3 class drones from 1 January 2026, extending to UK0 camera drones (100g+), UK4 model aircraft, and legacy aircraft from 1 January 2028
  • UK drone class marking rules (UK0/UK1/UK2/UK3/UK4) introduced from 1 January 2026
  • Green flashing light — mandatory for all Open Category drones flown at night from 1 January 2026
  • The Air Navigation Order 2016 (as amended)
  • Any local bylaws or restrictions at your flying location
  • The regulations of any country you are flying in, if outside the United Kingdom

Regulations are subject to change. Always refer to caa.co.uk/drones for the most current requirements and fees.

We make reasonable efforts to ensure accuracy, however:

  • Weather data is sourced from Open-Meteo and may not reflect hyperlocal conditions
  • KP index data is sourced from NOAA and may be subject to delays or temporary unavailability
  • Wind direction and speed data is sourced from Open-Meteo at 10m, 80m and 120m above ground level and may differ from conditions at your specific flying altitude
  • Golden hour calculations are mathematical estimates and may vary slightly from observed conditions

  • Offline cached data reflects conditions at the time of last successful data fetch and may be significantly outdated
  • CAA rules displayed are subject to change — always refer to caa.co.uk for current regulations
  • The 7-day best day to fly recommendation is based on forecast data which becomes less reliable further into the future
